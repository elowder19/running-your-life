The Point
Grossed out by your net pay? Every time you get paid, a chunk disappears before you ever see it. Federal tax, Kansas state tax, Social Security, Medicare — none of it is optional and none of it was explained to you when you got hired. Here is what actually happens to your paycheck — and why the people who set these rates matter more than most people realize.

Deductions — where it goes
Federal Income Tax
What is this? ▾
$0.00
Federal income tax is based on how you filled out your W-4 when you got hired. At part-time wages, most teenagers owe little to nothing in federal tax — especially if your total annual income is below the standard deduction of $16,100. If you claimed exempt on your W-4, this could be $0. This funds the federal government: roads, military, national programs.
Kansas State Income Tax
What is this? ▾
$0.00
Kansas uses a two-bracket progressive income tax system. In 2026: the first $23,000 of income is taxed at 5.20%, and anything above $23,000 is taxed at 5.58%. For most part-time workers making under $23,000/year, your effective rate is 5.20%. This funds Kansas state programs — schools, roads, and state services.
Social Security (FICA)
What is this? ▾
$0.00
6.2% of every dollar you earn goes here — no exceptions, no age limit, no opt-out. Social Security is a retirement program. You will not be able to collect this money until you are at least 67 years old. You are funding someone else's retirement right now. One day, someone else will fund yours.
Medicare (FICA)
What is this? ▾
$0.00
1.45% funds Medicare — healthcare for people over 65. There is no age exemption and no wage cap. You are paying into a healthcare program right now that you cannot use for approximately 50 years. Every American worker pays this. Not even teenagers get out of it.
