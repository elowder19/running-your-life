Your boss isn't your friend

Think about this first
Are you the main character at your job? Probably not.
The Point
Most people walk into their first job with a set of beliefs about how it works — some of them accurate, most of them not. Your boss is not your enemy, but they are also not your friend. They are running a business. Understanding what they actually care about — and what your actual rights are — is the difference between someone who keeps their job and someone who keeps losing them.
Myth or reality?

That is what your boss is actually tracking.
Reliability. Attitude when things go wrong. Whether you show up on time and do what you said you would do. Whether you cause drama or stay out of it. Everything else is noise. The employees who understand this tend to keep their jobs, get more hours, and get promoted. The ones who do not wonder why nothing ever works out.
