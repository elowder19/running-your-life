Your phone is lying to you

Think about this first
Everything you see online was put there by someone who wanted you to see it.
The Point
There are three kinds of content online: real, fake, and misleading. Fake is the easiest to catch — it is just wrong. Misleading is the dangerous one. It is often technically true, but framed to make you feel something before you think something. And that feeling is the whole point. Every headline below was designed to trigger a specific emotion. Your job is to name the emotion first — then decide if it is real, fake, or misleading.

Apply It
Next time something makes you angry, scared, or excited online — pause before you share it. Ask: what emotion is this designed to trigger? Who benefits if I feel that way? Can I verify this somewhere else? That three-second pause is the whole skill.
