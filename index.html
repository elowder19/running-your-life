Deductions — aka where it goes
Federal Income Tax
What is this? →
-$0.00
Federal income tax depends on how you filled out your W-4 when you got hired. At lower wages and part-time hours, this is often close to zero — but if you owe anything, it comes out here. This money funds the federal government: roads, military, national programs.
Kansas State Tax
What is this? →
-$0.00
Kansas takes 3.1% of your wages. This funds state programs — schools, roads, state services. Every Kansas worker pays this regardless of age.
Social Security (FICA)
What is this? →
-$0.00
6.2% goes here every single paycheck. This funds Social Security — a retirement program. You will not be able to collect this money until you are 67. Yes, you are paying into retirement right now. No, you cannot opt out.
Medicare (FICA)
What is this? →
-$0.00
1.45% funds Medicare — healthcare for people over 65. You are paying into a healthcare program right now that you cannot use for about 50 years. This comes out of every paycheck for every American worker. No exceptions. Not even for teenagers.
