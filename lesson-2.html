The Point
Your brain is an optimist. Every time you estimate how long something will take, it imagines the best case scenario — the one time it went perfectly. But your average morning is not your best morning. It is messier, slower, and full of things you did not plan for. This is called the planning fallacy — and it hits hardest when you are tired, rushing, and doing things on autopilot. Run through the rounds below. For each one — pick how long you think it actually takes.
